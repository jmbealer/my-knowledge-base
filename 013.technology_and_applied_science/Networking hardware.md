- Devices that mediate data transmission in a computer network
- ^^^**Networking hardware**^^^^^^, also known as^^^ ^^^**network equipment**^^^ ^^^or^^^ ^^^**computer networking devices**^^^^^^, are electronic devices that are required for communication and interaction between devices on a^^^ [computer network](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Computer_network)^^^.^^^
- ^^^ Specifically, they mediate^^^ [data transmission](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Data_transmission) ^^^in a computer network.^^^^[[1]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-IEEE_802.3-2012_Clause_9.1-1)^ 
- ^^^Units which are the last receiver or generate data are called^^^ [hosts](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Host_%28network%29)^^^,^^^ [end systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/End_system) ^^^or^^^ [data terminal equipment](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Data_terminal_equipment)^^^.^^^ 
- ## Range
    - [Size]();-[H2]()
- ^^^Networking devices includes a broad range of equipment which can be classified as core network components which interconnect other network components, hybrid components which can be found in the core or border of a network and hardware or software components which typically sit on the connection point of different networks.^^^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-2)^^[2](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-2)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-2)^
- ^^^One of the most common types of networking hardware today is a^^^ [copper-based Ethernet](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Copper-based_Ethernet) [adapter](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_interface_card) ^^^which is a standard inclusion on most modern computer systems.^^^ [Wireless networking](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/802.11) ^^^has become increasingly popular, especially for portable and handheld devices.^^^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-3)^^[3](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-3)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-3)^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-4)^^[4](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-4)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-4)^
- ^^^Other networking hardware used in computers includes^^^ [data center](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Data_center) ^^^equipment (such as^^^ [file servers](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/File_server)^^^,^^^ [database servers](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Database_server) ^^^and^^^ [storage areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Storage_area_network)^^^),^^^ [network services](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_service) ^^^(such as^^^ [DNS](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/DNS)^^^,^^^ [DHCP](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/DHCP)^^^,^^^ [email](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Email)^^^, etc.) as well as devices which assure^^^ [content delivery](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Content_delivery_network)^^^.^^^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-5)^^[5](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-5)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-5)^
- ^^^Taking a wider view,^^^ [mobile phones](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Mobile_phone)^^^,^^^ [tablet computers](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Tablet_computer) ^^^and devices associated with the^^^ [internet of things](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Internet_of_things) ^^^may also be considered networking hardware. As technology advances and^^^ [IP](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Internet_Protocol)^^^-based networks are integrated into building infrastructure and household utilities, network hardware will become an ambiguous term owing to the vastly increasing number of network-capable endpoints.^^^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-6)^^[6](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-6)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-6)^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-7)^^[7](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-7)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-7)^^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-8)^^[8](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-8)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-8)^
- ## Specific devices
    - [Size]();-[H2]()
- ^^^Network hardware can be classified by its location and role in the network.^^^
- ### ^^^**Core**^^^
    - [Size]();-[H3]()
- ^^^Core network components interconnect other network components.^^^
    - [Gateway](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Gateway_%28telecommunications%29): an interface providing a compatibility between [networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Computer_network) by converting transmission speeds, protocols, codes, or security measures.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-9)^^[9](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-9)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-9)^
    - [Router](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Router_%28computing%29): a networking device that forwards [data packets](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Data_packet) between computer networks. Routers perform the "traffic directing" functions on the [Internet](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Internet). A data packet is typically forwarded from one router to another through the networks that constitute the internetwork until it reaches its destination node.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-10)^^[10](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-10)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-10)^ It works on [OSI layer 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/OSI_model).^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-11)^^[11](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-11)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-11)^
    - [Switch](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_switch): a multi-port device that connects devices together at the same or different speeds on a computer network, by using [packet switching](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Packet_switching) to receive, process and forward data to the destination device. Unlike less advanced [network hubs](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Ethernet_hub), a network switch forwards data only to one or multiple devices that need to receive it, rather than broadcasting the same data out of each of its ports.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-12)^^[12](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-12)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-12)^ It works on [OSI layer 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/OSI_model).
    - [Bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_bridge): a device that connects multiple [network segments](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_segment). It works on [OSI layers 1 and 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/OSI_model).^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-13)^^[13](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-13)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-13)^
    - [Repeater](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Repeater): an electronic device that receives a [signal](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Signal) and retransmits it at a higher level or higher power, or onto the other side of an obstruction, so that the signal can cover longer distances.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-14)^^[14](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-14)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-14)^
    - [Repeater hub](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Repeater_hub): for connecting multiple [Ethernet](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Ethernet) devices together at the same speed, making them act as a single network segment. It has multiple [input/output](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Input/output)(I/O) ports, in which a [signal](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Signal) introduced at the input of any [port](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Computer_port_%28hardware%29) appears at the output of every port except the original incoming.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-IEEE_802.3-2012_Clause_9.1-1)^^[1](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-IEEE_802.3-2012_Clause_9.1-1)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-IEEE_802.3-2012_Clause_9.1-1)^ A hub works at the [physical layer](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Physical_layer) (layer 1) of the [OSI model](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/OSI_model)^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-15)^^[15](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-15)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-15)^ and all devices form a single [collision domain](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Collision_domain). Repeater hubs also participate in collision detection, forwarding a [jam signal](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Jam_signal) to all ports if they detect a [collision](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Collision_%28telecommunications%29). Hubs are now largely obsolete, having been replaced by [network switches](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_switch) except in very old installations or specialized applications.
    - [Wireless access point](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Wireless_access_point)
    - [Structured cabling](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Structured_cabling)
- ### ^^^**Hybrid**^^^
    - [Size]();-[H3]()
- ^^^Hybrid components can be found in the core or border of a network.^^^
    - [Multilayer switch](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Multilayer_switch): a [switch](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_switch) that, in addition to switching on [OSI layer 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/OSI_model), provides functionality at higher protocol layers.
    - [Protocol converter](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Protocol_converter): a hardware device that converts between two different types of [transmission](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Transmission_%28telecommunications%29), for interoperation.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-16)^^[16](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-16)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-16)^
    - [Bridge router](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Bridge_router) (brouter): a device that works as a bridge and as a router. The brouter routes packets for known protocols and simply forwards all other packets as a bridge would.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-17)^^[17](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-17)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-17)^
- ### ^^^**Border**^^^
    - [Size]();-[H3]()
- ^^^Hardware or software components which typically sit on the connection point of different networks (for example, between an internal network and an external network) include:^^^
    - [Proxy server](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Proxy_server): computer [network service](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_service) which allows clients to make indirect network connections to other network services.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-18)^^[18](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-18)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-18)^
    - [Firewall](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Firewall_%28networking%29): a piece of hardware or software put on the network to prevent some communications forbidden by the network policy.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-19)^^[19](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-19)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-19)^ A firewall typically establishes a barrier between a trusted, secure internal network and another outside network, such as the Internet, that is assumed to not be secure or trusted.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-Oppliger_1997_94-20)^^[20](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-Oppliger_1997_94-20)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-Oppliger_1997_94-20)^
    - [Network address translator](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_address_translator) (NAT): network service (provided as hardware or as software) that converts internal to external network addresses and vice versa.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-21)^^[21](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-21)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-21)^
    - [Residential gateway](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Residential_gateway): interface between a WAN connection to an [Internet service provider](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Internet_service_provider) and the [home network](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Home_network).
    - [Terminal server](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Terminal_server): connects devices with a serial port to a local area network.
- ### ^^^**End stations**^^^
    - [Size]();-[H3]()
- ^^^Other hardware devices used for establishing networks or dial-up connections include:^^^
    - [Network interface controller](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Network_interface_controller) (NIC): a device connecting a computer to a computer network.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-22)^^[22](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-22)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-22)^
    - [Wireless network interface controller](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Wireless_network_interface_controller): a device connecting the attached computer to a radio-based computer network.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-23)^^[23](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-23)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-23)^
    - [Modem](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Modem): device that modulates an analog "carrier" signal (such as sound) to encode digital information, and that also demodulates such a carrier signal to decode the transmitted information. Used (for example) when a computer communicates with another computer over a telephone network.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-24)^^[24](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-24)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-24)^
    - [ISDN terminal adapter](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/ISDN_terminal_adapter) (TA): a specialized [gateway](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Gateway_%28telecommunications%29) for ISDN.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-25)^^[25](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-25)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-25)^
    - [Line driver](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Line_driver): a device to increase transmission distance by amplifying the signal; used in base-band networks only.^[[](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-26)^^[26](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-26)^^[]](https://en.wikipedia.org/api/rest_v1/page/mobile-html/Networking_hardware#cite_note-26)^
