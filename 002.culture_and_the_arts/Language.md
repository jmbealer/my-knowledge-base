    - 0-100
        - **the**←** definite article** Refers to specific or already mentioned noun.
            - /ðə/ 
            - Please close the door. 
        - **be**←** verb** To exist, live, or occur.
            - /bi/ 
            - I will be there soon. 
        - **and**←** conjunction** Connects words, clauses, or sentences together.
            - /ænd/ 
            - I like apples and bananas. 
        - **of**←** preposition** Expresses relationship, possession, origin, or cause.
            - /ʌv/ 
            - It is a cup of tea. 
        - **to**←** preposition** Expressing motion or direction toward a point.
            - /tu/ 
            - We went to the store. 
        - **a**←** indefinite article** Used before a non-specific singular noun.
            - /ə/ 
            - I saw a bird flying. 
        - **in**←** preposition** Inside or enclosed by limits or boundaries.
            - /ɪn/ 
            - The cat is in the box. 
        - **have**←** verb** To possess, own, or hold something.
            - /hæv/ 
            - I have a new car. 
        - **it**←** pronoun** Refers to a non-human thing or animal.
            - /ɪt/ 
            - I threw the ball and caught it. 
        - **you**←** pronoun** Refers to the person being addressed.
            - /ju/ 
            - How are you doing today? 
        - **he**←** pronoun** Refers to a man, boy, or male animal.
            - /hi/ 
            - He is my younger brother. 
        - **for**←** preposition** Intended to benefit or belong to someone.
            - /fɔr/ 
            - This gift is for you. 
        - **they**←** pronoun** Refers to two or more people or things.
            - /ðeɪ/ 
            - They are playing soccer outside. 
        - **not**←** adverb** Used to make a word or statement negative.
            - /nɑt/ 
            - I do not like that color. 
        - **that**←** pronoun/conjunction** Identifies a specific person, thing, or fact.
            - /ðæt/ 
            - That is the car I want. 
        - **we**←** pronoun** Refers to the speaker and others.
            - /wi/ 
            - We are going to the park. 
        - **on**←** preposition** Supported by, attached to, or covering surface.
            - /ɑn/ 
            - The book is on the table. 
        - **with**←** preposition** Accompanied by; possessing; using or regarding.
            - /wɪð/ 
            - I went to lunch with her. 
        - **this**←** pronoun/adjective** Identifies a specific thing close by.
            - /ðɪs/ 
            - This is my favorite song. 
        - **I**←** pronoun** Refers to the person speaking or writing.
            - /aɪ/ 
            - I am very happy today. 
        - **do**←** verb** To perform an action or achieve something.
            - /du/ 
            - You must do your homework. 
        - **as**←** adverb/conjunction** Used to compare; while; because; like.
            - /æz/ 
            - He is as tall as me. 
        - **at**←** preposition** Expressing location, time, or a target.
            - /æt/ 
            - Meet me at the station. 
        - **she**←** pronoun** Refers to a female person or animal.
            - /ʃi/ 
            - She is reading a book. 
        - **but**←** conjunction** Used to introduce a contrast or exception.
            - /bʌt/ 
            - I am tired but happy. 
        - **from**←** preposition** Indicating the point of origin or source.
            - /frʌm/ 
            - I received a letter from him. 
        - **by**←** preposition** Near; through the agency or means of.
            - /baɪ/ 
            - The book was written by him. 
        - **will**←** auxiliary verb** Expresses future tense, desire, or determination.
            - /wɪl/ 
            - I will see you tomorrow. 
        - **or**←** conjunction** Connects alternatives, choices, or different possibilities.
            - /ɔr/ 
            - Do you want tea or coffee? 
        - **say**←** verb** To speak words or express a thought.
            - /seɪ/ 
            - What did you say to him? 
        - **go**←** verb** To move from one place to another.
            - /goʊ/ 
            - I need to go home now. 
        - **so**←** adverb/conjunction** To such a degree; therefore; very.
            - /soʊ/ 
            - I was so hungry I ate everything. 
        - **all**←** adjective/pronoun** The whole quantity or extent of something.
            - /ɔl/ 
            - We ate all the cookies. 
        - **if**←** conjunction** Introducing a condition or supposition; whether.
            - /ɪf/ 
            - Call me if you need help. 
        - **one**←** number/adjective/pronoun** The single, smallest whole number.
            - /wʌn/ 
            - I only have one apple left. 
        - **would**←** auxiliary verb** Expresses a condition, request, or past future.
            - /wʊd/ 
            - He said he would call tomorrow. 
        - **about**←** preposition/adverb** Concerning or approximately; nearly complete.
            - /əˈbaʊt/ 
            - syllable: a-bout 
            - Tell me about your trip. 
        - **can**←** auxiliary verb** Expresses ability, possibility, or permission.
            - /kæn/ 
            - She can swim very fast. 
        - **which**←** pronoun/adjective** Asking or referring to one choice.
            - /wɪtʃ/ 
            - Which book did you read? 
        - **there**←** adverb/pronoun** In or at that place or location.
            - /ðɛr/ 
            - We walked there quickly. 
        - **know**←** verb** To have information or understanding.
            - /noʊ/ 
            - Do you know how to fix this? 
        - **more**←** adjective/adverb** Greater in amount, number, or degree.
            - /mɔr/ 
            - Give me more details. 
        - **get**←** verb** To obtain, acquire, or become something.
            - /gɛt/ 
            - You should get a new coat.

        - **who**←**pronoun** Asking about or referring to a person.
            - /hu/
            - Who is knocking on the door?
        - **like**←**verb/preposition** To enjoy something; similar to something else.
            - /laɪk
            - I like to read books.
        - **when**←**adverb/conjunction** At what time; at the time that.
            - /wɛn/
            - When will you arrive at home?
        - **think**←**verb** To use the mind to form ideas.
            - /θɪŋk/
            - I think that is a good idea.
        - **make**←**verb** To create, form, or cause to exist.
            - /meɪk/
            - She can make a delicious cake.
        - **time**←**noun** The indefinite continued progress of existence.
            - /taɪm/
            - What time does the movie start?
        - **see**←**verb** To perceive with the eyes; to understand.
            - /si/
            - I can see the mountain from here.

    - **Example Clarifications:**

    - Acronym vs. Initialism:
        - Acronym: Pronounced as a word (e.g., NASA, laser).
        - Initialism: Pronounced as letters individually (e.g., FBI, ATM).
    - 
    - Homophones: Words pronounced similarly but differ in meaning/spelling (e.g., “to,” “two,” “too”).
    - Homographs: Words spelled the same but have different meanings, sometimes different pronunciations (e.g., “lead” (to guide) vs. “lead” (metal)).
    - Polysemy: A single word having multiple related meanings (e.g., “bank” as financial institution and riverbank).
    - Portmanteau: A word blending sounds and meanings of two others (e.g., brunch = breakfast + lunch).
    - Palindrome: Words or phrases reading the same forward and backward (e.g., radar, civic).
